Vikings never wore horned helmets. Not in battle, not ever.

There is no archaeological evidence for horned Viking helmets — real ones were simple iron or leather caps. The horned image was popularised in the 1800s, especially by costumes for Wagner's operas, and it stuck. Horns on a helmet would have been clumsy and dangerous in a real fight. The look is pure 19th-century romanticism.
