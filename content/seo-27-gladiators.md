## Description
```
The Colosseum, year 80 AD. Two gladiators step onto the sand — and what happens next is the only gladiator duel from ancient Rome ever described in detail. This is their story, and the story of who the gladiators really were.

Forget the Hollywood version. They weren't all doomed slaves butchered for fun. The real gladiators were the celebrities of their age — feared and adored — trained like elite athletes, fed a special "barley men" diet, treated by the finest doctors in the empire, and most of them, fight after fight, actually lived. Some were prisoners with no choice. Others were free men who volunteered for the glory and the gold. We follow the whole journey: from the chains and the terrible oath, through the brutal school, the fighting types, the truth about thumbs and "those who are about to die," all the way to the legendary duel of Priscus and Verus — and the wooden sword of freedom.
```
