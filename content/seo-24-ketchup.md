Ketchup was once sold as medicine. In the 1830s, an American doctor decided tomatoes could cure illness — and sold tomato ketchup as pills.

He claimed it could treat indigestion, diarrhoea, and even jaundice. For a while, "tomato medicine" became a genuine craze, with rivals making wilder and wilder claims. The problem: many pills didn't actually contain much tomato, and the whole thing collapsed into scandal. Ketchup went back to being a condiment — and we mostly forgot it was ever a so-called cure.
