## Description
```
Ancient Romans wiped with a shared sponge on a stick. It was called a tersorium.

In public toilets it was the standard tool — rinsed in a channel of running water, or a bucket of vinegar or salt water, then left for the next person. Yes, the next person. Roman public latrines were communal in every sense: long benches with rows of holes and no privacy, where people sat side by side and chatted. Some historians think the shared sponge helped spread disease.
```
