## VO (EN)
> "In ancient Egypt, if your cat died, you shaved off your eyebrows. And you weren't being dramatic — it was the custom of grief. The Greek historian Herodotus wrote that when a household cat died of natural causes, everyone living there shaved their eyebrows in mourning. When a dog died, they shaved their whole body. Cats were sacred to the goddess Bastet, guardians of the home and the grain stores. Killing one, even by accident, could be punished by death. Beloved cats were mummified, and archaeologists have found cemeteries with hundreds of thousands of cat mummies. So those bare eyebrows were a public badge of grief — proof you'd lost a member of the family. Follow for more history you won't believe is real."
