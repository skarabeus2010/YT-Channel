The samurai weren't all men. For centuries, Japan had female warriors of the samurai class — the onna-bugeisha.

Trained to defend their homes and clans, they fought especially with the naginata, a long pole topped with a curved blade. History records women like Tomoe Gozen, described in medieval accounts as "a warrior worth a thousand," riding into battle and taking heads like any male samurai. Recent studies of battlefield remains suggest women may have fought far more often than the legends alone admit.
