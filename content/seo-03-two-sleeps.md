## Description
```
For most of history, humans slept twice a night — a "first sleep" and a "second sleep," with a waking hour around midnight.

Before artificial light, this two-part night was completely normal. Historian Roger Ekirch found over 2,000 references to it. People used the quiet hour to pray, talk, or reflect on their dreams — until gas and electric light squeezed sleep into a single block.
```
