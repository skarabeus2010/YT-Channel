The ancient Egyptians invented a pregnancy test that actually sort of worked.

Around 3,500 years ago, a woman who thought she might be pregnant would urinate on bags of wheat and barley seeds over several days. If the seeds sprouted, she was said to be pregnant. Astonishingly, when modern scientists tested the idea, the urine of pregnant women really did make the seeds grow more often — probably because of elevated hormones. The sex-prediction part doesn't hold up, but the basic test was thousands of years ahead of its time.
