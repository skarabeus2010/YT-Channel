## VO (EN)
> "For most of human history, nobody slept through the night — and that was completely normal. Before artificial light, people slept in two shifts. They'd go to bed after dark for a 'first sleep,' wake around midnight for an hour or two, then return for a 'second sleep' until dawn. And that waking hour wasn't wasted — people prayed, talked, tended the fire, visited neighbours, or just lay thinking about their dreams. The historian Roger Ekirch found more than two thousand references to this two-part night, going back centuries. It only faded when gas and electric light pushed bedtimes later and squeezed sleep into a single block. So if you wake at 3 a.m. — your body may just be doing what it did for thousands of years. Follow for more forgotten history."
