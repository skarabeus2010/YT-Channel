Samurai armour was not a heavy metal shell — it was built for speed.

Instead of a single solid plate, it was made from hundreds of small scales of iron and leather, coated in tough lacquer to resist water and rust, then laced together with silk cords. The result was strong, flexible and surprisingly light, letting a samurai move, ride and draw a bow with ease — often far lighter than a European knight's plate. It was also a thing of beauty, lacquered in bold colours that signalled a warrior's clan.
