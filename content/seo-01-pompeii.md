```
The last day of Pompeii began like any other — bread in the oven, election posters on the walls, a green mountain no one feared.

This is the story of a single ordinary day in the Roman town of Pompeii, reconstructed from what the ash preserved: the roughly 35 bakeries (one found with 81 loaves still in the oven), the 150 fast-food counters, the graffiti-covered walls — and the mountain that the locals didn't even recognize as a volcano. We follow an ordinary baker through the morning, then watch the day turn through the eyes of the only surviving witness, Pliny the Younger, who saw the giant "umbrella pine" cloud rise around one in the afternoon. From the rain of pumice to the deadly pyroclastic surges of the night, this is how a living city became the most famous snapshot in history.
```
