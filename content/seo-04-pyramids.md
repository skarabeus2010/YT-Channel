How the pyramids were built has puzzled people for centuries — and the real answer is just water and sand, not aliens.

Ancient Egyptians moved millions of multi-ton stone blocks across the desert on wooden sledges. On dry sand, the sledge plows up a heap that makes pulling almost impossible. Their fix was deceptively simple: pour water in front of the sledge. The right amount of moisture binds the sand grains, keeps the surface firm, and dramatically cuts the friction — a technique modern physicists measured in 2014. An ancient Egyptian tomb painting even shows a worker pouring water ahead of a sledge hauling a colossal statue. No lost technology, no aliens — just brilliant practical engineering.
