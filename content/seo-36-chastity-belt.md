The medieval chastity belt is almost certainly a myth.

You know the image — a knight rides off to the Crusades and locks his wife into an iron contraption. But there's basically no evidence medieval people used them. The famous museum examples were mostly made centuries later, in the 1700s and 1800s, as jokes, fakes or curiosities, and many have quietly been removed from display. A real iron belt worn for years would have caused fatal infections. It tells us more about later ages than about the Middle Ages.
