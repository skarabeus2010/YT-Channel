## VO (EN)
> "Picture a Viking, and you see a warrior in a horned helmet. There's just one problem: Vikings never wore them. Not a single horned helmet has ever been found in a Viking grave. In a real battle, horns would be useless — easy to grab, and a gift to your enemy. So where did the image come from? An opera. In eighteen seventy-six, a costume designer for Wagner's Ring cycle gave the Norse characters horns to make them look wild and ancient. The look stuck, and within decades everyone 'knew' Vikings wore horns. Real Viking helmets were simple rounded caps of iron. The horns are pure theatre — about as historical as a cowboy with a lightsaber. Follow for more myths busted."
