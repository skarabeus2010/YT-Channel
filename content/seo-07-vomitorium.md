## Description
```
A "vomitorium" was not a room where Romans threw up to keep eating. That's a total myth.

A vomitorium was actually an architectural feature: the passageway that let crowds "spew" quickly out of an amphitheatre like the Colosseum. The name comes from the Latin for "to disgorge" — the building disgorged its spectators. The puking-room idea was invented much later and stuck because it's gross and memorable. The real Romans would be baffled by it.
```
