We tell it like a tragedy in a single night: the greatest library in the ancient world burning in one catastrophic blaze. It's mostly a myth.

The Library of Alexandria didn't die in a fire — it died slowly, over centuries, of neglect: lost funding, expelled scholars, wars, decay, and scrolls no one re-copied. Caesar's fire, the Serapeum's destruction, and the later Muslim-conquest legend are each blamed, but historians doubt any one of them destroyed it in a stroke. The real loss is sadder, and the real lesson is about how fragile knowledge truly is.
