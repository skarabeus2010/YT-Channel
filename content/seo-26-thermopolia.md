## Description
```
Ancient Rome was full of fast-food joints. They were called thermopolia — and a city like Pompeii had dozens of them.

Each had an L-shaped stone counter with large jars set into it, holding hot, ready-to-eat food and drink. Poorer Romans often had no kitchen at home, so they ate out constantly — stews, bread, cheese, wine and snacks on the go. One thermopolium uncovered in Pompeii even had colourful paintings advertising the menu, with traces of the actual food still inside the jars. Street food, 2,000 years ago.
```
