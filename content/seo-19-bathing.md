Medieval people were not filthy. The "dirty Middle Ages" is almost entirely a myth.

Public bathhouses were everywhere — a large medieval town could have dozens, where people soaked, socialised and washed several times a week. Soap was made and sold, and being clean meant being respectable. They washed their hands before and after meals, since forks weren't common. The real decline in bathing came later, after the Middle Ages, when people feared open pores spread the plague.
