Napoleon wasn't actually short. The tiny, raging emperor is one of history's most successful insults.

He was recorded at 5 feet 2 — but in old French inches, which were longer than English ones. Converted properly, he stood around 5'6" to 5'7", completely average for a Frenchman of his time. The myth came partly from British propaganda cartoons, partly from his nickname "the little corporal" (a term of affection), and partly because he was usually surrounded by his tall imperial guard.
