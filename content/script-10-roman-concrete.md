## VO (EN)
> "We build with concrete that crumbles in fifty years. The Romans built with concrete still standing after two thousand — and it actually heals itself. The secret was hidden in tiny white chunks of lime scattered through the material. For years, scientists thought these were just sloppy mixing. Then, in twenty twenty-three, researchers at MIT found the truth: when a crack forms and water seeps in, those lime chunks dissolve and react, growing new crystals that seal the crack shut. The concrete repairs its own wounds. The Romans even used volcanic ash and seawater, which made their harbour concrete stronger over time, not weaker. We're only now reverse-engineering what they did by hand. Sometimes the future is buried in the past. Follow for more ancient genius."
