We build with concrete that crumbles in 50 years. The Romans built with concrete still standing after 2,000 — and it heals itself.

The secret was tiny white chunks of lime in the mix. For years scientists thought these were sloppy mixing. Then, in 2023, MIT researchers found the truth: when a crack forms and water seeps in, those lime chunks dissolve and react, growing new crystals that seal the crack shut. The Romans also used volcanic ash and seawater, which made their harbour concrete stronger over time.
