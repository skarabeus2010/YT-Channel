The Mongols built a postal system that could outrun an empire — the Yam.

It was a vast network of relay stations spread across thousands of miles, each stocked with fresh horses, food and shelter. A messenger could gallop into a station, leap onto a waiting horse and ride on without delay, covering astonishing distances — by some accounts hundreds of miles in a day. Riders wore bells so the next station could ready a horse before they arrived. Centuries before the telegraph, the Mongols had near-instant communication.
