## VO (EN)
> "You've probably heard that ancient Romans had special rooms called vomitoria, where they threw up between courses to keep eating. It's one of history's most famous facts — and it's completely wrong. A vomitorium was never a puke room. It was the passage that let a packed amphitheatre spew out its crowd quickly after a show. The Colosseum had dozens, emptying fifty thousand people in minutes. The word is Latin for 'to discharge' — the building vomited people, not the other way around. The myth only appeared in modern times, when writers misread the name. So no, Romans weren't all binge-and-purge gluttons. Their architecture was just ahead of ours. Follow for more history that's stranger than fiction."
